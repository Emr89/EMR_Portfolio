2020 Supplemental Football Match Up Analysis

April 11, 2017

Section Explanation

This section has two subsections. The first subset, “Outcome Prediction”, which was created in the “Football Strategy Project”, gives the performance predictions for the probability of success for the Patriots in their matchup against the 49ers. The Last subset,“Strategic Play Analysis”, introduces new supplementary statistical models that were created to help the Patriots maximize their efficiency and greatly increase their chances of success. These models can look at factors for individual plays to determine which rush direction or pass level would greatly benefit the Patriots on offense, given the circumstances and goal in mind.

The second section will have several options based on the play callers’ goal. For example, the models will give a suggestion for the least risky pass play (highest completion estimation), but will also give an separate suggestion for a pass play that takes the estimated completion probability into account, along with the average amount of yards estimated for that play. Each scenario will produce 4-5 separate suggestions, which are created for the separate goals of the play callers.

Note: For the second subsection, only 4 play scenarios, one scenario for each team on offense for run and pass, were given. The reasons are the following: After taking into account all the possible combinations off the factors, the total number of combinations would be ~ 896,000 predictions for the rushing game, and ~ 768,000 for the passing game MINIMUM (As I am only including up to 10 yards for the yards to go factor). Dumping all 1.5+ million predictions would have been a waste of time and effort for both parties, as this section is only meant to convey this subsections’ capabilities.

New Supplementary Statistical Models Explained: For the second section, supplementary advanced statistical models were created for the Patriots to maximize the efficiency of their offense and strategize more effectively to the strengths of their defense. The models were created at the play by play level, therefore the models take the following factors into account: Quarter of the game, Down of the play, Yards to go, the matchup of teams on the offensive and defensive side of the ball, the formation used, the rush direction, if rush, the pass type (depth and side) if pass, and the yard line the ball is on. The models measure how the relationships of all those factors impact the following: how many yards are gained on the play (on average), the probability that the play goes for a first down, the probability that the play goes for a touchdown, and the expected formation and rush direction/pass type that any team would most likely use in any situation (given the down of the play, the quarter of the game, the amount of yards to go for the first down, the teams of both sides of the ball, and the yard line the offense is on). These set of models will be referenced as “PAD (PLAY ANALYSIS DECISIONS)”.

After applying the most advantageous models to the 2020-week 7 matchup versus the San Francisco 49ers, the 49ers would have been slightly favored to win the match up with a win probability of ~ 55.628% (NE win probability is 44.372%. The score on average, would have been ~ 22 – 24 on average, which means the Patriots would have scored ~ 3 touchdowns, with a probability of ~ 0.33 of scoring one additional field goal, and the 49ers would have scored ~ 3 touchdowns and an additional field goal, with a slight probability of scoring ~ 4 touchdowns. The Patriots would have passed for ~ 195.8629 yards and ~ 1-2 touchdowns after 30/31 attempts. The Patriots would have rushed for ~147.9138 yards, with a probability of ~ 63.31% of scoring a rushing touchdown. The remaining probability for any unallocated points would have been a defensive score or made field goals. The Patriots’ offense would have had ~ 21/22 first downs, and ~ 2 turnovers (interception or fumble, ~ 83.56% chance being atleast one interception). The Patriots’ defense would have allowed ~ 20/21 first downs, with ~ 218.2353 passing yards, ~ 113.4144 rushing yards, and would have probably come away with ~ 1 takeaway (60.5467% chance of an interception). The 49ers’ offense would have passed for ~ 2 touchdowns after 33/34 attempts. The 49ers’ offense would have had a 51.2767% chance of scoring a rushing touchdown after 15/16 attempts. If the 49ers did not score a rushing touchdown, it would have been a defensive score or a pair of field goals.

Strategic Play Analysis:

49ers’ Rush Example: In this scenario, the 49ers will have the football, and we will assume that it is going to be a rushing play. The scenario is that it is the 1st quarter, its 3rd down with 3 yards to go, on their own 35-yard line.

The predictions for all combinations of Rush Direction and Formation are the following:

Best Option Considering Yards, Touchdown Probability, and First Down Probability (FTY): DRNE_SFD32578

This is the best play considering the average amount of yards, the probability of scoring a touchdown, and the probability of getting a first down. The situation entails having the QB under center and handing off the ball to the right end for an average of ~ 7.60 yards, and a ~ 45.84% chance of getting a first down. This is the safest of all the risky plays, as it maximizes the chance of scoring a touchdown and a big gain, while it minimizes the probability of having the play blown up in the backfield. Additionally, when looking at the range of the probabilities of getting a first down, this probability is around average, which is beneficial considering that the average amount of yards is way above average for all possible combinations. This play would be most effective for a gamble rushing play.

Best Option Considering Yards, and First Down Probability (FY): DRNE_SFD17890

This is the best play considering the average amount of yards and the probability of getting a first down. This situation entails the QB going under center and directing the rushing play off the right tackle for an average of 8.30 yards, with a 45.14% chance of getting a first down. This play would be suggested for a risky rushing play to get a much needed first down, as this predicted amount of yards is the highest for all conditions, while still having an around average probability of getting a first down.

Best Yards Option: DRNE_YDS17890

As stated above, the average amount of yards is maximized in these conditions, so it would be suggested for the getting the longest possible gain if you believe the circumstances and player matchups are in your favor.

Best Touchdown Option: DRNE_SFD32578

This play is best for maximizing the probability of scoring a touchdown in this situation, though going for the touchdown in those conditions would not be advised as it would be the first quarter and the probability of scoring a touchdown would only be ~1.33%. Though this play would most likely get a first down if needed. The situation, as stated above for FTY, would entail the QB being under center, and handing off the ball to the right end for an average of ~ 7.60 yards, with a 45.85% chance of getting a first down.

Best First Down Option: DRNE_SFD26458

This play is the best play for maximizing the probability of getting a first down. Additionally, this play would also be the most strategically effective play given the circumstances. The play entails the offense going no huddle, with the QB in shotgun, and handing off the ball for a rush directed towards the left guard. The play would average around 5.11 yards, with ~ a 50.60% chance of getting a first down (which is the highest) but would only have a 0.46% chance of getting a touchdown.

Models’ Predicted Rush Direction and Formation Option for 49ers in This Situation:

In these conditions, the 49ers are expected to run the ball through the center, with the second most likely direction would be off the right guard. By far, the most likely formation would be under center in that situation for the 49ers.

Under Center, Center Run Prediction:

If the 49ers ran the expected play, they would average ~ 5.31 yards, but would only have ~ a 42.93% chance of getting a first down, which is below average considering the range.

Under Center, Right Guard Run Prediction:

If the 49ers were under center and ran off the right guard, they would average ~ 5.71 yards, and would have ~ a 46.78% chance of getting a first down, which would be a more effective play call for them.

Utilization of Strategic Play Prediction In-Game: Though the examples used in the “Strategic Play Analysis” section appear to be a lengthy process that would not be suitable for in-game use, the examples were heavily overstated to convey an understanding of how to utilize the predictions created. The PAD system can easily be organized effectively into a system that can be utilized in-game. This process would include organizing the table of all predictions in such a way that it can easily be navigated to get a comparison of all play options at a moments’ notice. This would include dividing sections by quarter and by down. Additionally, the play callers’ “goals” would be given a symbol/phrase representation to save time. These symbols could be anything because they are arbitrary. But as an example, the following could be utilized: Alpha = first down probability priority, Beta = yards average priority, Gamma = touchdown probability priority, Delta = Completion Probability priority, Epsilon = YTC priority (Yards & Touchdown & Completion), etc. Currently, an organized excel file with all predictions is in progress for any future matchup. In-game use is possible for this option if there is an individual up in the box, using a data sorting tool (probably excel) to quickly sort and report the best play option given the play callers’ goal. Additionally, a “GUI”, which is just an organized search engine that programmers created a dweeby name for, can be created within a couple weeks to automatically sort through all the predictions. So, future in-game strategic suggestions would be almost instantaneous.
